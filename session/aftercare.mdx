Proper aftercare is the single most important thing you can do after your Inkbot session. Inkbot's specialized ink formula is engineered for exceptional color retention and longevity — but only when the healing process is supported correctly. How you treat your skin in the days and weeks following your session directly determines how vibrant, sharp, and long-lasting your tattoo will be. Follow this protocol closely and contact your studio technician if anything concerns you.

## The first 24 hours

The first day is the most critical window for your tattoo's healing. Follow these steps in order:

<Steps>
  <Step title="Keep the protective film on">
    Leave the protective film your technician applied on for **4–6 hours** after your session. It shields the fresh tattoo from airborne bacteria and physical contact while initial healing begins.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Remove the film gently">
    After 4–6 hours, remove the film **under lukewarm running water**. Let the water loosen the adhesive edges — do not peel the film away dry, as this can pull at the skin.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Pat dry — do not rub">
    Gently pat the area dry using a **clean paper towel**. Do not use a cloth towel (which can harbour bacteria) and never rub the tattooed skin. Rubbing disrupts the ink and damages healing tissue.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Apply aftercare balm">
    Apply a **thin, even layer** of Inkbot Aftercare Balm. If you don't have the Inkbot balm, a fragrance-free, alcohol-free moisturizer is an acceptable substitute. Use only a small amount — a thick layer prevents the skin from breathing.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Leave it uncovered">
    Do **not** rewrap or re-bandage your tattoo unless your technician has specifically instructed you to do so. Your skin needs airflow to heal properly.
  </Step>
</Steps>

## Days 2–14

During the peeling and settling phase, your routine should be consistent and gentle:

- **Wash gently twice daily** using unscented, mild soap and lukewarm water. Do not scrub — use light circular motions with clean fingertips.
- **Apply aftercare balm 2–3 times per day** to keep the skin moisturized. Dryness leads to excessive peeling, which can pull ink out of the skin prematurely.
- **Avoid submerging in water.** No baths, swimming pools, hot tubs, or open-water swimming until the tattoo is fully healed. Brief showers are fine.
- **Keep the tattoo out of direct sunlight.** UV exposure during healing can cause significant color fading and uneven healing. Cover the area with clothing if you're going outdoors.
- **Do not scratch or pick at peeling skin.** Peeling is a normal part of healing. Let it happen naturally — picking or scratching removes ink along with the dead skin and can create patchy results.

## Long-term care

Once your tattoo is fully healed — typically **2–4 weeks** after your session — the day-to-day care becomes much simpler. The single most effective thing you can do to preserve color vibrancy over the long term is apply **SPF 50+ sunscreen** to the tattooed area whenever it's exposed to sunlight. UV radiation is the primary cause of tattoo fading, and consistent sun protection will keep your Inkbot tattoo looking fresh for years.

If you ever feel your tattoo has faded or needs refreshing, **annual touch-ups are available at any certified Inkbot studio**. Touch-ups are a quick session that restores line definition and color saturation to keep your design looking its best.

## Signs to watch for

<Warning>
  Most tattoos heal without complications, but contact a medical professional promptly if you notice any of the following, as they may indicate an infection:

  - **Redness spreading beyond the tattoo border** — some redness immediately around the area is normal in the first 48 hours, but spreading redness is not
  - **Swelling lasting more than 72 hours**
  - **Pus, oozing, or unusual discharge** from the tattooed area
  - **Fever** or general feelings of illness following your session

  Do not attempt to treat a suspected infection at home. Seek medical attention and notify your Inkbot studio so they can follow up.
</Warning>

## Frequently asked questions

<AccordionGroup>
  <Accordion title="When can I exercise after my session?">
    Wait at least **48–72 hours** before returning to exercise. Sweating introduces bacteria to the healing skin, and physical movement can stretch or irritate the tattooed area. When you do resume exercise, rinse the tattoo gently with clean water afterwards and reapply your aftercare balm. Avoid contact sports or any activity with a high risk of direct impact to the tattooed area until you're fully healed (2–4 weeks).
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="Can I swim or use a hot tub?">
    No — avoid all submersion in water until your tattoo is fully healed. This includes baths, swimming pools, the ocean, and hot tubs. Submerging a healing tattoo exposes it to bacteria, chlorine, salt, and other chemicals that can cause infection, color loss, and uneven healing. Showers are fine as long as you don't direct a strong stream of water directly onto the tattoo. Most customers can safely return to swimming after **3–4 weeks**, once the skin has fully closed and healed.
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="How do I know if I'm having an allergic reaction?">
    Mild itching and redness directly around the tattoo are normal during healing and are not signs of an allergic reaction. An allergic reaction typically presents as **intense or spreading itchiness**, a **raised rash or hives** around or beyond the tattoo area, **unusual swelling**, or **blistering**. If you experience any of these symptoms — especially if they appear several days after your session or seem to be getting worse rather than better — stop using any products on the area and seek medical advice. Inform your doctor that Inkbot uses a specialized ink formula; your studio can provide the full ingredient list if needed.
  </Accordion>
</AccordionGroup>
