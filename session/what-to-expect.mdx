Knowing what to expect before you walk through the door makes for a smoother, more comfortable experience. Your Inkbot studio session is a structured process guided by a certified technician — from the moment you check in to the final aftercare briefing. This guide walks you through every stage so there are no surprises on the day.

## Before you arrive

A little preparation goes a long way toward making your session as comfortable and efficient as possible:

- **Eat a meal beforehand.** Arriving on an empty stomach can make you feel lightheaded during the session. Have a proper meal at least an hour before your appointment.
- **Avoid alcohol for 24 hours prior.** Alcohol thins the blood and can increase sensitivity. Skip it the day before your session.
- **Wear loose or easily removable clothing.** Choose clothing that gives your technician easy access to the target area without requiring a full wardrobe change. For example, wear a tank top for shoulder work or shorts for calf designs.
- **Arrive 10 minutes early.** This gives you time to check in, complete any paperwork, and settle in before the session begins — without rushing.

## Your session step by step

<Steps>
  <Step title="Check-in">
    Present your **booking confirmation QR code** and a **valid photo ID** to the studio technician at the front desk. Your appointment will be confirmed in the system and you'll be shown to your session area.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Consultation">
    Your technician reviews your saved Print Configuration with you, discusses placement in detail, and answers any questions you have. This typically takes **5–10 minutes**. Speak up if you have any last-minute changes to positioning or sizing — this is the right moment to raise them.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Skin preparation">
    The technician cleans the target area thoroughly and shaves it if needed to ensure a smooth, clean printing surface. This step is quick and painless.
  </Step>
  <Step title="3D body scan">
    If a scan of your target area hasn't been captured previously, the technician performs one now. The scan takes approximately **30 seconds** and allows the robotic arm to precisely map contours for accurate ink placement.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Final preview approval">
    A digital overlay of your design is displayed on a tablet, mapped to your actual body contours. Review the placement, scale, and orientation carefully, then give your verbal confirmation to proceed. No printing begins until you approve.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Printing">
    The robotic arm applies your tattoo using Inkbot's precision micro-needle array. Depending on the size and complexity of your design, printing takes approximately **20–60 minutes**. You'll need to remain as still as possible during this phase.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Post-print review">
    Once printing is complete, the technician inspects the output alongside you under proper lighting. If any minor corrections are needed — edge definition, color density — they are addressed at this stage.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Aftercare briefing">
    The technician applies a **protective film** over your fresh tattoo and walks you through the full aftercare protocol. Make sure you understand every step before you leave. You'll also receive a printed or digital aftercare guide.
  </Step>
</Steps>

## Pain and sensation

Inkbot uses a micro-needle array system that is functionally similar to traditional tattooing — needles deposit ink beneath the skin's surface using rapid, precise movements. The sensation you'll experience is comparable to a conventional hand-applied tattoo: a persistent scratching or stinging feeling that varies in intensity based on placement. Areas over bone (ribs, ankles, spine) tend to be more sensitive than fleshier areas.

Most customers find the experience very manageable, especially for small to medium designs. If you have a low pain threshold or are particularly sensitive in the target area, **numbing cream is available on request**.

<Tip>
  Request numbing cream when booking your session so the studio technician can prepare and apply it before your appointment begins — it needs approximately 45 minutes to take full effect.
</Tip>

## Session duration

Total time at the studio depends on your design's size and complexity. Use the table below as a general guide. Allow extra time for check-in, consultation, and aftercare briefing on top of the print time shown.

| Design size | Approximate print time |
|---|---|
| Small (under 5 cm) | 20–30 minutes |
| Medium (5–15 cm) | 30–50 minutes |
| Large (over 15 cm) | 50–90 minutes |

<Note>
  Complex multi-color designs typically fall toward the upper end of the range for their size category. If your design features fine linework, gradients, or four or more colors, plan for additional time.
</Note>
