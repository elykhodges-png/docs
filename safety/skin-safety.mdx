Inkbot takes safety seriously. This page explains what happens behind the scenes to keep every session safe — from the ink formulas we use to how we sterilize equipment between customers.

## Ink composition

Our inks are formulated to meet the highest safety standards available in the tattoo industry. Here's what goes into every Inkbot session:

- **FDA-cleared ink formula** — every color in our palette has been reviewed and cleared for use in cosmetic tattooing.
- **Hypoallergenic by default** — our standard formula is designed to minimize the risk of allergic reactions for most skin types.
- **Free of nickel, mercury, and lead** — no heavy metals are used at any concentration in Inkbot inks.
- **Available in 96 colors plus black and white** — choose from our full palette using the design tool in the Inkbot app.
- **Vegan-friendly** — all Inkbot inks are free of animal-derived ingredients and are not tested on animals.
- **Patch testing available on request** — if you have a history of skin sensitivities or ink reactions, request a patch test when booking via **Dashboard → Book Session → Special Requests**.

## Equipment sterilization

Every piece of equipment that comes into contact with your skin is either single-use or fully sterilized before your session begins.

- **All needle arrays are single-use and sealed** until your session — they are opened in front of you and disposed of immediately afterward.
- **The robotic print head undergoes a full autoclave sterilization cycle** between each customer — the same sterilization standard used in surgical and dental settings.
- **All contact surfaces are sanitized with medical-grade disinfectant** before and after every session, including the support armature, positioning frame, and studio chair.

<Note>
  You have every right to ask the studio technician to show you the sealed needle packaging before your session begins. Our technicians are happy to confirm the lot number and seal integrity on request.
</Note>

## Contraindications

Certain medical conditions and circumstances mean that tattooing — including Inkbot's robotic process — may not be safe for you at this time.

<Warning>
  **Do not book a session if any of the following apply to you:**

  - You are **pregnant or breastfeeding**
  - You are currently taking **blood thinners** (including warfarin, aspirin therapy, clopidogrel, or similar medications)
  - You have **active eczema, psoriasis, or dermatitis** in or near the target area
  - You have a **known allergy to tattoo ink** or any of its typical components
  - You have a **history of keloid scarring** — tattooing may trigger keloid formation
  - You have undergone **radiation therapy on the target area** within the past 12 months
</Warning>

<Info>
  If any of the above conditions apply to you — even partially — consult your physician before booking. Always disclose relevant medical conditions to your studio technician during the pre-session health check. This information is kept confidential and is used only to ensure your safety.
</Info>

## Age requirements

<Warning>
  **Inkbot sessions are available to customers aged 18 and over in most jurisdictions.** Customers aged 16–17 may be eligible for a session where permitted by local law, provided they bring a completed written consent form signed by a parent or legal guardian. The consenting guardian must be present at the studio at the time of the session.

  Age verification is required for all customers. Bring a valid government-issued photo ID to your appointment. Sessions will be refused if valid ID cannot be produced.
</Warning>

## Skin condition requirements

The area of skin you want tattooed must be in a suitable condition on the day of your session. The target area must be free of:

- **Open wounds or broken skin** of any kind
- **Active rashes, hives, or allergic reactions**
- **Severe sunburn** — wait until the skin has fully healed before booking
- **Significant hair growth** that cannot be shaved — the studio technician can assist with shaving the area if needed, but heavy growth may affect scan accuracy and ink adhesion

If you are unsure whether your skin is in a suitable condition, contact support at [support@inkbot.io](mailto:support@inkbot.io) or use the live chat in the Inkbot app before your appointment.
