Inkbot makes it easy to bring your artwork into the system so you can place, preview, and print it with precision. Upload your design directly from the dashboard, make any last-minute adjustments in the built-in design studio, and save everything to your personal Design Library for use at any Inkbot studio location.

## Supported file formats

Inkbot accepts three file formats for tattoo artwork. All formats share the same 50 MB file size limit.

| Format | Max Size | Notes |
|--------|----------|-------|
| SVG    | 50 MB    | Recommended for scalability |
| PNG    | 50 MB    | 300 DPI or higher recommended |
| PDF    | 50 MB    | First page only is imported |

<Tip>
  SVG files are the best choice for crisp, scalable designs. If you're working with raster artwork, ensure your PNG is exported at 300 DPI or higher before uploading. For detailed preparation advice, see the [Design Guidelines](/design/design-guidelines).
</Tip>

## Uploading a design

<Steps>
  <Step title="Open the Designs section">
    Log in to your Inkbot account and navigate to **Dashboard → Designs**.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Start the upload">
    Click the **Upload** button in the top-right corner. In the upload dialog, either drag and drop your file onto the target area or click **Browse** to select a file from your device.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Add a title and tags (optional)">
    Give your design a recognisable title and add optional tags (for example, `floral`, `sleeve`, `blackwork`) to make it easier to find later. Both fields can be edited at any time from your Design Library.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Save to your library">
    Click **Save**. Inkbot processes and stores your file. Once the upload completes, your design appears in your Design Library and is ready to place on a body map or preview.
  </Step>
</Steps>

## Design studio adjustments

After uploading, open any design in the Design Studio to fine-tune its appearance before placement. The following adjustments are available:

- **Contrast** — Increase or decrease the tonal range to make your design stand out against skin.
- **Brightness** — Lighten or darken the overall image to compensate for very dark or very light source artwork.
- **Saturation** — Boost or reduce color intensity to match your desired vibrancy.
- **Linework conversion** — Convert a raster or illustrated design into a clean linework style. This is useful for simplifying complex artwork or achieving a traditional tattoo aesthetic.

All adjustments are non-destructive. Your original uploaded file is always preserved, and you can reset to defaults at any time.

<Info>
  Free tier accounts can store a maximum of **100 designs** in the Design Library. To store more, upgrade to an Inkbot Pro or Studio plan. Designs that exceed the limit are not deleted — you'll simply be prompted to upgrade or remove existing designs before adding new ones.
</Info>
