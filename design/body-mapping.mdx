Body mapping is the step that ensures your design is applied at exactly the right position, scale, and orientation on your skin. Rather than relying on hand-drawn stencils or flat templates, Inkbot builds a precise 3D model of your actual skin surface. This model becomes the canvas on which you place and preview your design digitally before a single drop of ink is deposited.

## How the scan works

<Steps>
  <Step title="Arrive at the studio">
    When you arrive for your appointment, a technician will guide you to the scanning area. Wear or bring clothing that provides easy access to the body zone being tattooed — for example, a sleeveless top for upper arm work or shorts for calf work.
  </Step>
  <Step title="The technician positions the scanner">
    The technician positions Inkbot's structured-light 3D scanner around the target body area. You don't need to do anything at this stage — just relax in a natural, comfortable posture. The scanner emits harmless patterned light (not a laser) to measure the contours of your skin surface.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Stay still for the scan">
    The scan takes approximately **30 seconds**. Hold as still as possible during this time. Breathing normally is fine; avoid shifting weight, turning, or tensing muscles, as movement introduces distortion into the surface model.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Review your 3D model">
    Once the scan is complete, a 3D model of your skin surface is generated and loaded automatically into your active session in the Inkbot app. You and your technician can review the model together on the studio display before proceeding to placement and preview.
  </Step>
</Steps>

## Privacy and data

Your body scan captures detailed geometric data about your skin surface. Inkbot takes this seriously.

Scan data is used exclusively for your current session — it is not shared with third parties, used for training, or transferred to any other system. By default, all scan data is **permanently deleted within 72 hours** of your session ending.

If you would like to reuse your body scan for future sessions (for example, to add to a sleeve over multiple visits), you can opt in to saving the scan to your Inkbot account. You can delete saved scans at any time from **Account → Privacy → Body Scan Data**.

<Note>
  Scan data is stored on Inkbot's encrypted servers in your region and is subject to our full data retention and deletion policy. If you do not opt in to saving your scan, it is automatically purged within 72 hours and cannot be recovered. You can review your data preferences at any time under **Account → Privacy**.
</Note>

## Supported body zones

Inkbot's robotic arm is calibrated to work accurately across the following body zones:

- **Arms** — Upper arm, lower arm (forearm), or full arm
- **Legs** — Thigh, calf, or full leg
- **Back** — Upper back, lower back, or full back
- **Chest**
- **Abdomen**
- **Shoulder**
- **Neck** — Available with placement restrictions; consult your technician

<Warning>
  **Face, scalp, hands, and feet require a medical clearance consultation before any Inkbot session can be booked.** These zones involve thinner skin, higher nerve density, or areas with unique healing characteristics. A qualified practitioner will review your suitability and discuss any additional risks before proceeding. Contact your nearest Inkbot studio to arrange a consultation.
</Warning>

## Skin tone and texture

Inkbot's structured-light scanner is designed to work accurately across **all skin tones**, including very light and very deep complexions. The scanner measures surface geometry rather than skin color, so melanin levels do not affect scan accuracy.

If you have **textured or scarred skin** in the target area — including raised keloid scarring, significant stretch marks, or surgical scarring — please inform your technician before the scan begins. These surfaces can still be scanned successfully, but the technician may need to perform additional calibration passes to ensure the surface model is accurate. This adds a few minutes to the preparation time. Inkbot's system will also automatically flag areas of high surface variation in the placement tool so you can position your design to best complement or work around the texture.
